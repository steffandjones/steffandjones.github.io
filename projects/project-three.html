Balsa Wood Bridge

Designed and built a balsa wood bridge optimized for load-bearing strength using a tension-based string system. Images and final documentation below.

This project involved designing and building a small-scale truss bridge entirely from balsa wood. The goal was to achieve the highest load-to-weight ratio possible using only basic materials and adhesives.

The final design used a triangular truss structure and integrated a tension-based string reinforcement system to distribute force more effectively and reduce compression in weak points. Strings were strategically placed underneath the main span to transfer tension into the base, preventing sagging under load.

We iteratively tested joint strength and geometry through physical prototyping and refined our layout based on observed stress failures. The final bridge held over 250 lbs, despite weighing less than 200 grams — a massive ratio and a highlight of lightweight structural design.

From this project, I gained hands-on experience in structural mechanics, tension/compression dynamics, and practical lessons in material behavior, bonding techniques, and failure analysis.
