My Internships

During my time at Alesi Surgical Ltd., I worked as a mechanical engineering intern supporting product testing, design optimization, and rapid prototyping for next-generation laparoscopic devices. On this page you can find images of what I did, as well as a link to Alesi Surgical's own website.

Alesi Surgical is a medical device company based in Cardiff, Wales, focused on developing innovative technologies to improve surgical outcomes. Their flagship product, Ultravision™, is a pioneering system used in laparoscopic surgeries to manage surgical smoke and maintain clear visibility. As a mechanical engineering intern at Alesi Surgical, I worked closely with the engineering team to support product testing, design refinement, and quality improvement efforts across multiple devices.

One of my main responsibilities was evaluating and improving the structural rigidity of a laparoscopic instrument in response to feedback from surgeons. Working alongside two other engineers, I conducted a series of mechanical tests using an Instron machine to quantify flexural strength across various rod materials. After gathering performance data, I compiled results into Excel spreadsheets, generated visualizations, and documented findings in formal reports. Our analysis ultimately influenced which material was selected for use in the final production model.

Midway through my internship, I was asked to support the Ultravision team with a separate challenge: redesigning their internal test rig to reduce the time required to verify each device. I was given full creative control over the design process, using SolidWorks to model and iterate multiple prototypes. After several rounds of 3D printing and refinement, I delivered a final version that significantly streamlined the testing workflow. In addition to these projects, I also helped maintain internal documentation by checking and updating Bills of Materials (BOMs). I thoroughly enjoyed working across disciplines and contributing to both hands-on testing and product development.
