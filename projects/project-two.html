3D Holographic Fan

Built a 3D LED fan display as part of my final Electrical Engineering project. The device used a rotating 1D LED strip to render any image in midair, on demand, through persistence of vision. Images and articles can be found below.

This was my final project for my Intro to Electrical Engineering class. The goal was to build a rotating 1D LED strip that could render images in midair using persistence of vision. The inspiration came from a similar display I saw at a Kung Fu Tea shop, displaying their delicious boba tea - and I wanted to build one entirely from scratch.

The system used an Arduino microcontroller to map any uploaded image to timed RGB flashes as the strip spun. My team and I wrote custom code to calculate the correct color and timing based on the LED’s radial position and rotation speed, depending on the requested image.

Fabrication included 3D printing, soldering, breadboarding, and integrating a rechargeable battery (and the breadboard) directly onto the rotating body to avoid slip rings or tangling wires. I also designed a protective case for the electronics and motor(to avoid any accidents) and worked towards finding the correct current input to achieve our desired RPM.

Through this project, I learned how to synchronize fast hardware timing with mechanical systems, design for motion, and debug both hardware and code under a 3 week time constraint. It was a creatively fulfilling challenge that brought together all aspects of electrical and mechanical engineering. It was fun to be able to display any image we were asked to, from the Harvard crest to all sorts of different emojis!
