3D Holographic Fan

Built a 3D LED fan display as part of my final Electrical Engineering project. The device used a rotating 1D LED strip to render 2D images in midair through persistence of vision. Images and documentation are shown below.

This was my final project for Electrical Engineering. The goal was to build a rotating 1D LED strip that could render images in midair using persistence of vision (POV). The inspiration came from a similar display I saw at a Kung Fu Tea shop — and I wanted to build one entirely from scratch.

The system used an Arduino microcontroller to map any uploaded image to timed RGB flashes as the strip spun. I wrote custom code to calculate the correct color and timing based on the LED’s radial position and rotation speed. The rotating arm was designed in SolidWorks, with attention to balance, power routing, and wireless power.

Fabrication included 3D printing, soldering, and integrating a rechargeable battery directly onto the rotating body to avoid slip rings or tangling wires. I also designed a protective case for the electronics and optimized the LED placement for maximum display area.

Through this project, I learned how to synchronize fast hardware timing with mechanical systems, design for motion, and debug both hardware and code under constraints. It was a creatively fulfilling challenge that brought together all aspects of electrical and mechanical engineering.
